# ISO 27005 — Information Security Risk Management

## What is ISO 27005?
ISO 27005 is the international standard that provides guidelines for **information security risk management**. It supports ISO 27001 
by giving organizations a structured way to identify, analyze, evaluate and treat risks.

## The Risk Management Process

### Step 1 — Context Establishment *(Établissement du contexte)*
Understand the organization : its objectives, environment, and what it needs to protect.

### Step 2 — Risk Identification *(Identification des risques)*
Identify all assets, threats that could affect them, and vulnerabilities that threats could exploit.

### Step 3 — Risk Analysis *(Analyse des risques)*
For each risk, estimate :
- **Likelihood** *(Probabilité)* — How likely is it to occur?
- **Impact** *(Impact)* — What are the consequences?
- **Risk level** = Likelihood × Impact

### Step 4 — Risk Evaluation *(Évaluation des risques)*
Compare each risk level against the organization's 
**risk criteria** to decide which risks are acceptable.

### Step 5 — Risk Treatment *(Traitement des risques)*

#### Avoid *(Éviter)*
**Definition:** Eliminate the risk by stopping the activity that generates it.
*(Éliminer le risque en arrêtant l'activité qui le génère.)*

**Principle:** If a risk is too high and cannot be reduced sufficiently, the safest option is to simply not do the activity.
*(Si un risque est trop élevé et ne peut pas être suffisamment réduit, l'option la plus sûre est de ne pas faire l'activité.)*

**Example:** A company decides not to store customer credit card data to avoid any risk of financial data breach.
*(Une entreprise décide de ne pas stocker les données de carte bancaire des clients pour éviter tout risque de fuite financière.)*

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Reduce *(Réduire)*
**Definition:** Implement security controls to lower the likelihood or impact of the risk.
*(Mettre en place des contrôles de sécurité pour réduire la probabilité ou l'impact du risque.)*

**Principle:** The risk cannot be avoided but can be made acceptable by applying technical or organizational measures.
*(Le risque ne peut pas être évité mais peut être rendu acceptable en appliquant des mesures techniques ou organisationnelles.)*

**Example:** Installing antivirus software, enabling multi-factor authentication, training employees on phishing.
*(Installer un antivirus, activer l'authentification multi-facteurs, former les employés au phishing.)*

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Transfer *(Transférer)*
**Definition:** Share or shift the financial consequences of a risk to a third party.
*(Partager ou transférer les conséquences financières d'un risque à un tiers.)*

**Principle:** The risk still exists but another party bears the financial burden if it occurs.
*(Le risque existe toujours mais une autre partie supporte le fardeau financier s'il se produit.)*

**Example:** Taking out cyber insurance or outsourcing a critical service to a certified provider.
*(Souscrire une assurance cyber ou externaliser un service critique à un prestataire certifié.)*

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

#### Accept *(Accepter)*
**Definition:** Acknowledge the risk and decide to take no action, because the cost of treatment exceeds the potential impact.
*(Reconnaître le risque et décider de ne prendre aucune mesure, car le coût du traitement dépasse l'impact potentiel.)*

**Principle:** Acceptance must be a conscious and documented decision, not an oversight. The residual risk must be within the organization's risk appetite.
*(L'acceptation doit être une décision consciente et documentée, pas un oubli. Le risque résiduel doit être dans l'appétit au risque de l'organisation.)*

**Example:** A low-probability risk with minimal impact is documented and monitored without additional controls.
*(Un risque de faible probabilité avec un impact minimal est documenté et surveillé sans contrôles supplémentaires.)*

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

| 🇬🇧 Option | 🇫🇷 Traduction | Key idea |
|---|---|---|
| **Avoid** | Éviter | Stop the risky activity |
| **Reduce** | Réduire | Apply security controls |
| **Transfer** | Transférer | Cyber insurance / outsourcing |
| **Accept** | Accepter | Document & monitor |

## Risk Appetite & Risk Tolerance

### Risk Appetite *(Appétit au risque)*
The amount of risk an organization is **willing to accept** in pursuit of its objectives.
*(La quantité de risque qu'une organisation est prête à accepter pour atteindre ses objectifs.)*

**Example:** A startup accepts high risk to innovate fast. 
A bank has very low risk appetite due to sensitive financial data.

### Risk Tolerance *(Tolérance au risque)*
The **acceptable variation** around the risk appetite — the boundaries within which the organization can operate.
*(La variation acceptable autour de l'appétit au risque — les limites dans lesquelles l'organisation peut opérer.)*

| | 🇬🇧 English | 🇫🇷 Français |
|---|---|---|
| **Risk Appetite** | How much risk we *want* | Combien de risque on *veut* |
| **Risk Tolerance** | How much risk we *can handle* | Combien de risque on *peut supporter* |

## Qualitative vs Quantitative Analysis

### Qualitative Analysis *(Analyse qualitative)*
Uses **descriptive scales** to assess likelihood and impact such as Low / Medium / High.

**Risk Matrix *(Matrice des risques)* :**

| | Low Impact | Medium Impact | High Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High Likelihood** | 🟡 Medium | 🔴 High | 🔴 Critical |
| **Medium Likelihood** | 🟢 Low | 🟡 Medium | 🔴 High |
| **Low Likelihood** | 🟢 Low | 🟢 Low | 🟡 Medium |

✅ Fast and easy to understand
❌ Subjective, no precise numbers

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Quantitative Analysis *(Analyse quantitative)*
Uses **numerical values** to calculate risk, typically expressed in financial terms.

**Key formulas :**
```
ALE = ARO × SLE

ALE = Annual Loss Expectancy      (Perte annuelle estimée)
ARO = Annual Rate of Occurrence   (Taux d'occurrence annuel)
SLE = Single Loss Expectancy      (Perte par incident)
```

**Example :**
- Data breach costs **€50,000** (SLE)
- **20% chance** of occurring per year (ARO = 0.2)
- **ALE = 0.2 × 50,000 = €10,000 per year**

✅ Precise and objective
❌ Complex, requires historical data

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

| | Qualitative | Quantitative |
|---|---|---|
| **Measure** | Low / Medium / High | Numbers, euros |
| **Speed** | Fast | Slow |
| **Precision** | Approximate | Precise |
| **Best for** | Small organizations | Large organizations |

## Residual Risk & Risk Acceptance

### Residual Risk *(Risque résiduel)*
The risk that **remains after** security controls have been applied.
No control eliminates risk completely.
```
Residual Risk = Initial Risk − Effect of Controls
```

**Example:** Installing antivirus reduces malware risk 
but never to zero → residual risk remains.

### Risk Acceptance *(Acceptation du risque)*
The formal decision to **acknowledge and live with** the residual risk,because treating it further is too costly or impractical.

⚠️ Acceptance must always be :
- **Documented** *(Documentée)*
- **Approved by management** *(Approuvée par la direction)*
- **Regularly reviewed** *(Réévaluée régulièrement)*

## ISO 27005 ↔ ISO 27001

*"ISO 27001 requires organizations to perform risk management. 
ISO 27005 explains how to do it."*
```
ISO 27001  →  The WHAT  (Quoi faire)
ISO 27005  →  The HOW   (Comment le faire)
```
```
ISO 27001
│
├── Clause 6.1 — Risk Management (mandatory)
│         │
│         └──► ISO 27005 provides the methodology
│
└── Annex A — Security Controls
          │
          └──► Selected based on ISO 27005 results
```
